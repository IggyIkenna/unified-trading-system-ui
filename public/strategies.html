Allocation process

How it works

From initial conversation to live capital — a clear, regulated process with full transparency at every step.

Discovery call

We scope your objectives, risk appetite, and regulatory requirements. No commitment needed.

Strategy fit

We recommend a strategy mix based on your goals. You review historical performance, risk metrics, and operational details.

Regulated onboarding

Professional-client classification, compliance checks, and mandate documentation — all under FCA oversight.

Live allocation

Capital deployed. You get a client portal with live positions, P&L attribution, risk reporting, and settlement tracking.

What allocators get

Every mandate comes with regulated reporting, operational oversight, and full platform visibility.

Regulated structure

FCA-authorised investment management. Professional-client reporting, oversight, and allocation discipline. Engagement runs on a twelve-month minimum.

Client portal

Live positions, P&L attribution, trade history, compliance reports, settlement tracking, and fee transparency — the same surface Odum uses internally, view-filtered to the allocator.

Research-to-live continuity

The same strategy logic runs from research through paper trading to live deployment. Daily reconciliation confirms alignment.

Fund or SMA — you choose the structure

Allocate through a fund (share-class-sliced view per allocator; fund administration handled by a regulated affiliate) or through a separately managed account under your own structure. Both run on the same reporting surface and the same strategy code.

Odum never custodies client capital

Custody sits with you (SMA) or with the fund's regulated administrator (fund structure). Odum is never the custodian in either structure. For SMA mandates, you fund a venue account in your name and issue Odum a scoped API key with execute and read permissions only — no withdrawal authority, ever, and revocable at any time. Keys live in Odum's Secret Manager and are hot-reloaded into execution at runtime.

Fees disclosed at second call

FCA-regulated; performance-fee mechanics and platform-access mechanic are disclosed at the second call alongside the mandate structure.
