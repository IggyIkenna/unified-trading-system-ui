First time here?

This looks really complex. Where do I start?

It is complex — and pretending otherwise would be dishonest. Unifying five asset categories, the full trading lifecycle, multiple regulatory shapes, and a commercial structure that scales from a single signal handoff to a full investment mandate is genuinely a lot of ground.

Our job is to meet you where you are and accelerate you through the complexity without overloading the first conversation. Three things carry most of that weight:

  • The floating help chat (bottom-right on every page) — a curated decision tree that routes you to the right deep dive, questionnaire, or contact path depending on what you’re after. No LLM, no guesswork, no bouncing around the site.
  • The ~2-minute questionnaire — six quick questions about which asset classes, venues, and strategy styles fit you. Once that’s on file, every subsequent conversation is pre-configured to your shape; you don’t re-explain yourself on the call.
  • The platform itself is modular — backend and front-end are built so each client sees only the surfaces relevant to their path. Investment Management clients don’t see developer docs; DART Full clients don’t see umbrella workflows. Scope narrows by design once we know who you are.

If you have two minutes, start with the questionnaire. If you’d rather just talk, book a 45-minute call. Either way, everything below this question is here for the moments you want specifics.

The product

What does Odum actually do?

We run a unified trading operating system across five asset categories — crypto, DeFi, traditional markets, sports, prediction markets — and offer it commercially through four paths: Investment Management, DART (the full platform), Odum Signals (our signals, your execution), and the Regulatory Umbrella.

How is this different from [Deltix / Talos / QuantConnect / CoinRoutes / Amberdata / Hummingbot / TradingView / Waystone…]?

There isn’t a single vendor that’s a direct comparable — the market is fragmented by layer, and each specialist is usually excellent at their piece. The gap we fill is between the pieces. Layer by layer:

  • Data & market intelligence — Amberdata, Tardis, Databento. Best-in-class normalised feeds and derivatives / DeFi analytics. We source from this tier rather than compete with it; what we add on top is a research and execution stack that consumes clean data end-to-end.
  • Research / backtest / strategy development — Deltix QuantOffice, QuantConnect / LEAN. Strong quant environments. We extend further: strategies you build on DART run against the same live execution and reporting layers Odum uses for its own capital, across all five asset categories, and without migrating between backtest and live.
  • Execution & smart order routing — Talos, CoinRoutes, Versify, Aplo, Deltix CryptoCortex, Interactive Brokers. Institutional execution and venue connectivity that does its job well. We integrate with — not replace — the venues; the Odum difference is that execution sits inside the same operating system as research, risk, and reporting. No vendor stitching.
  • Charting & terminal UX — TradingView. The best charting UX in the business. We still use it for some of our own charts. It was never trying to be an operating layer.
  • Open-source automation — Hummingbot. Excellent modular automation framework for crypto market making; different audience, serves operators who want to run their own infrastructure.
  • Regulatory platforms / hosted-manager structures — Waystone, FundRock. Deep fund-structure and AIFM / ManCo services we respect. Our Regulatory Umbrella is narrower in ambition — designed for trading firms that want a quick route to operate regulated activity under Odum’s permissions, on the same operating stack as our IM clients.

The unified-vs-fragmented frame: those specialists are usually sold separately, and clients stitch them together. We offer one operating system across all six layers plus the five asset categories. The advantage is less about beating any single specialist on their strongest lane — several of them are genuinely better at that lane — and more about eliminating the vendor stitching, duplicated workflows, and inconsistent UX that come with assembling a stack yourself.

The posture is partner-first, not conquest-first. Several of the firms above are in our own supplier stack, and we’d happily operate as an affiliate or partner to any of them where capabilities complement rather than overlap.

IP, ownership, partnership

How do you protect our strategy IP?

Whenever a conversation moves to strategy, the first real question is about IP. Execution-only firms exist because routing trades on your behalf isn’t IP-sensitive — you’re the broker’s client; they do what you instruct. Handing over your research code is a different conversation. DART splits that conversation into two modes; you pick the one that fits where your edge lives.

DART — Signals In (your code stays yours): you keep the strategy in your own codebase. We never see the code and never see how you make money. What you use is the platform, reporting, regulatory coverage, data pipelines, and execution stack — the infrastructure pieces that are expensive to build from scratch. Your signal goes in; the rest of the pipeline does the job.

DART — Full (build with us on a locked-off strategy): for firms that want the interconnectedness of the full pipeline — data through research through execution through reporting through regulatory. A first call maps your needs; we match to the closest strategy in our catalogue and tailor it. Whatever we build together is locked off — it doesn’t enter our investment-management book, we don’t trade it ourselves, and it stays exclusive to you for the length of the contract.

The catalogue sits behind the bespoke tailoring conversation, not the marketing page. It holds ~2,500 strategy combinations across the five categories, and we don’t walk through it on day one. First the questionnaire, then an initial fit call, then the demo — only once the fit is clear do we open the catalogue and tailor from it. That’s not coy; it’s how trust is built, and it protects the clients who have already locked off their piece.

Either way, we are not competing with you. The whole product is structured so we can’t. Deeper: DART Signals-In deep dive and DART Full deep dive.

Why don’t you just trade all the strategies yourselves?

The obvious challenge: you’ve seen the catalogue — raise a few billion and run them all. The numbers don’t work.

We do trade our catalogue ourselves. Week by week we take strategies from cold through lukewarm to live — the final rounds of live-capital validation. But the catalogue is a catalogue precisely because the infrastructure makes it possible, not because we’ve personally traded every combination. Even at a two-week pace per strategy — faster than anything we’ve seen in production — ~2,500 strategies is 5,000 weeks, roughly 100 years of single-desk work. That’s before adding the team, the sales force, and the operational capacity to raise and run capital against each one.

Partnership is how the maths works. If a hundred partners each run $100M of Odum-backed strategies, we reach $10B under management without raising another pound — and every partner is an extension of the platform whilst we're not even 10% of the way there in terms of coverage potential. Genuine affiliation, aligned through revenue-share. When partners grow, we grow. That’s the dual incentive, and it’s load-bearing.

What happens when a strategy decays?

Anyone who has actually traded knows strategies decay. That’s a fact of the market, not a flaw in the firm. What matters is the rate at which you can iterate and the platform around each strategy.

Every strategy on the platform starts with a baseline, moves through backtest, paper-traded validation, and eventually live-traded results. Strategies evolve — v1, v2, v3 — the same way any serious trader evolves their book. Clients can and should evolve theirs. The theoretical cap on strategy count across the taxonomy is vast, and we’re nowhere near it.

Regulatory & compliance

Are you regulated?

Yes — FCA-authorised (FRN 975797), regulated since January 2023. Client scope: professional clients and eligible counterparties under FCA rules. We don’t offer services to retail investors.

How do you handle sanctions — Iran, Russia, and similar jurisdictions?

Compliance with UK, EU, and US sanctions regimes is built into the operating model. We screen for sanctioned jurisdictions and entities at onboarding and on an ongoing basis; we do not onboard clients subject to sanctions, and we do not route orders or deliver signals to venues or counterparties we can’t reach compliantly. This is owned by our compliance function and reviewed regularly against FCA and OFAC guidance.

Can I operate under your FCA permissions?

Yes, via the Regulatory Umbrella. Firms that want to conduct regulated activities without the 12–18 month wait for direct FCA authorisation can operate as an Appointed Representative or under an advisory structure. See the Regulatory Umbrella deep dive for the shapes and the onboarding flow.

Getting started

How do I read the deep dives?

The deep dives are lightly gated with a shared access code. Ask us for one via contact or on a 45-minute call — we issue codes to qualified prospects within a working day.

What’s the flow to become a client?

Six steps, most of them quick. The sequencing is deliberate — each step tailors the next one so we’re not repeating ground on later calls.

  • 1. Deep dives (optional, access code) — if you want depth before we talk, request an access code and read the deep dive that matches your path.
  • 2. Questionnaire (~2 min) — which asset classes, venues, and strategy styles fit you. The better we understand you here, the more tailored the first call.
  • 3. Initial call (~30 min) — fit discussion. We confirm Odum is the right shape for your need and that we’ve understood enough to tailor the right product.
  • 4. Demo — we show you the platform in action first; then you run it freely yourself and decide whether the value’s there.
  • 5. Bespoke tailoring — if the lights are green, we open the ~2,500-combination catalogue and tailor from it: strategies, infrastructure, regulatory posture.
  • 6. Signup + go live — entity details for contract generation, compliance sign-off, email verification. When everything moves fast, same week as the bespoke conversation is realistic.

Full workflow with SLAs and data contracts: signup / signin workflow playbook .

Do I need to book a demo before signing up?

Yes — signup sits near the end of the funnel, not the start. Everyone goes through the questionnaire → initial call → demo → bespoke tailoring sequence first so we can configure the right platform surfaces, API keys, rate limits, and contract scope for you. Short-circuiting to signup before the demo would mean provisioning blind; the flow above exists so we don’t.

Why is the sign-in locked?

Accounts are created disabled by default. Our compliance team enables them after KYC / AML review and contract signing — usually within a couple of working days of the demo. You’ll receive an email verification link at that point.
