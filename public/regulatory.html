Reporting surface — inherited from the operating stack

What you get from day one — reporting and analytics

Regulatory Umbrella clients get the full Odum reporting and analytics tool-set the same way Investment Management and DART clients do. Not a separate regulatory-reporting product; a partition of the same operating system, entitlement-filtered to your regulated-activity view.

Positions, P&L, performance

Positions, exposures, P&L attribution, performance breakdowns, reconciliation, audit trail, MiFID II and transaction reporting, supervisory artefacts. Same components IM allocators and DART operators use.

Read-only venue API keys

Onboarding wires your venues in via read-only API keys you provide. Odum pulls position, order, and fill data from your venues into the shared reporting surface. No capital moves to Odum; no execution runs through Odum's venues. You keep full operational control.

Funds, sub-clients, sub-mandates

The same keys let you build up funds, sub-clients, and sub-mandates under your firm and track their performance and breakdowns in the platform. Pooled or SMA structure, your downstream client hierarchy, Odum's reporting surface.

Why structure matters

If you are managing capital, executing on behalf of clients, or moving from tooling into decision-making, the regulatory line can become much thinner than it first appears. Commercial agreements, software terms, and advisory labels do not by themselves replace the need for the right structure where activities are in scope.

Firms usually come to us for one of four reasons:

Moving into regulated territory

They are moving from infrastructure or signals into activities that may be regulated.

Speed to market

They want to manage third-party capital without waiting 12–24 months for direct authorisation.

Established framework

They would rather operate under an established framework than build compliance, reporting, and oversight from scratch.

Credibility and distribution

They want the credibility of operating and promoting under a regulated structure when speaking to clients, counterparties, and distribution partners.

Onboarding process

From inquiry to operational

A structured process that gets you live quickly. Timeline depends on the scope and complexity of your proposed activities, typically ranging from weeks to a few months.

1

Eligibility assessment

We scope your proposed activity, client base, and regulatory requirements to confirm fit. No commitment — takes 1–2 calls.

2

Due diligence

Background checks, AML/KYC documentation, and business plan review. Typically 1–2 weeks depending on documentation readiness.

3

Structuring & agreement

We determine the right engagement structure based on your proposed activities, client base, and regulatory scope. You receive a clear agreement covering permissions, compliance support, reporting infrastructure, and supervision — all under Odum's existing FCA authorisation.

4

Go live

Begin regulated operations with full compliance support, supervision, and reporting. Ongoing annual review and monitoring included.
