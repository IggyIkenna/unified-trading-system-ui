2024 · Summer

HFT lesson lands; unified stack build begins

Two and a half years in, the HFT journey runs its known arc. AUM grows from the initial $100k to $3.5M by Q1 2024, and then the environment shifts. Extremely easy at the start. Very hard at the end. The market matures, edges compress, and the research pipeline starts to decay faster than it can be rebuilt. The lesson lands: build an arsenal of strategies rather than ride a single one; reduce sole reliance on latency — compete on smarts, not just speed; get a top-down view of the full market universe before committing research time.

The accelerator thesis emerges from that lesson. The hunt was for someone else’s accelerator for our own journey — a stack that could carry the whole market in one taxonomy — and it ended with the realisation nobody was building it.

Summer 2024: we start building it ourselves. Institutional-grade infrastructure — single source of truth for data contracts, config-hashed versioning, shard-level failure isolation, simulation equals live — extends across every asset category. One strategy-instruction protocol covers CeFi, DeFi, TradFi, sports, and prediction; the catalogue grows into the thousands of combinations. With breadth like that, growing with others starts to look smarter than being the centre of our own universe.

First run is for ourselves — the same stack that will be offered to clients carries our own live capital end-to-end. Client paths get structured early: partners can build on the platform; strategies built together stay exclusive to that engagement; everyone grows without competing.

Roughly 14 months after founding, Odum Research Ltd is authorised by the Financial Conduct Authority. The HFT crypto strategy goes live under the new licence; first client capital arrives — starting modestly at $100k. Compliance, reporting, and client oversight are built into the operating model from day one — not retrofitted later. Client scope: professional clients and eligible counterparties.

Odum launches. The first two and a half years are a focused HFT crypto arbitrage journey: year one builds the strategy core while the FCA authorisation runway runs in parallel. Crypto-focused throughout — HFT arbitrage being a market teh founder knew well from his time at IMC.

From 2011

Trading desks — IMC, then Mako Pedigree

From 2011, Ikenna trades at IMC (Amsterdam) and Mako (London). Volatility arbitrage and systematic market-making across European and US indices; builds and runs eight-figure-revenue desks at both firms. Earlier exposure to Morgan Stanley and Goldman Sachs preceded that — the prop path was a deliberate choice for the meritocracy and tech-first culture over the bulge bracket.

Self-taught code along the way, formalised with a data-science course in New York that sharpened the machine-learning half — well before AI assistants existed. Across the career, desks have traded crypto, commodities, equities, and interest rates — single-name and index, OTC and electronic, high-frequency and medium-frequency, predictive signal and liquidity provision. That cross-market breadth is why one stack can credibly treat every category as a single taxonomy.
