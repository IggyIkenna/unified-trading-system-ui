How Odum came to be — dated.

The moments that shaped Odum — the trading desks that came before, FCA authorisation, the build, the commercial launch. It’s a living page; it grows as we do.

2026 · Spring

All four commercial paths live Now

Investment Management, DART, Odum Signals, and Regulatory Umbrella are all serving clients. The same operating system powers every path — research, execution, risk, and reporting share one code base. Categories covered end-to-end: crypto exchanges, DeFi protocols, traditional markets, sports, and prediction markets.

From 2025

Team grows from solo to five

Odum moves from a solo build into a five-person team. Rob Osborne joins as CFO & Adviser; Harsh Kantariya leads Quant Engineering; Julian John runs Programme & Operations; Femi Amoo builds core infrastructure. Quant research, engineering, programme, and finance are now in place alongside the CEO.

2024

Unified stack reaches full breadth

Institutional-grade infrastructure — single source of truth for data contracts, config-hashed versioning, shard-level failure isolation, batch equals live — extended across every asset category. One strategy-instruction protocol covers CeFi, DeFi, TradFi, sports, and prediction. The catalogue grows into the thousands of strategy combinations.

January 2023

FCA authorisation FRN 975797

Roughly 14 months after founding, Odum Research Ltd is authorised by the Financial Conduct Authority and begins managing live capital as an investment manager. Compliance, reporting, and client oversight are built into the operating model from day one — not retrofitted later. Client scope: professional clients and eligible counterparties.

2021

Odum founded Solo build

Odum begins as a solo effort. The first fourteen months are spent building the strategy core and the business — cross-asset from the start, spanning crypto, crypto ETFs, and traditional markets — while the FCA authorisation runway proceeds in parallel.

Pre-Odum

Two and a half years of HFT crypto arbitrage The origin

The seed of Odum: two and a half years of high-frequency cross-exchange arbitrage in crypto. Extremely easy at the start. Very hard at the end. The market matures, edges compress, and the research pipeline starts to decay faster than it can be rebuilt. Lesson: build an arsenal of strategies rather than ride a single one; cheapen the stack by stripping latency dependence; get a top-down view of the full market universe before committing research time.

From 2011

Trading desks — IMC, then Mako Pedigree

From 2011, Ikenna trades at IMC (Amsterdam) and Mako (London). Volatility arbitrage and systematic market-making across European and US indices; builds and runs eight-figure-revenue desks at both firms. Earlier exposure to Morgan Stanley and Goldman Sachs preceded that — the prop path was a deliberate choice for the meritocracy and tech-first culture over the bulge bracket.

Self-taught code along the way, formalised with a data-science course in New York that sharpened the machine-learning half — well before AI assistants existed. Across the career, desks have traded crypto, commodities, equities, and interest rates — single-name and index, OTC and electronic, high-frequency and medium-frequency, predictive signal and liquidity provision. That cross-market breadth is why one stack can credibly treat every category as a single taxonomy.
